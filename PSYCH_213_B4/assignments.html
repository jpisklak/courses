Course Project I: Qualitative Research in Psychology

Due Date: April 10th (last day of classes) by 23:59
Time Remaining: 

For this project, you will watch an informative lecture by Dr. Michael Sharp from the University of Alberta's Psychology Department (the video is linked below). This lecture introduces you to qualitative methodologies in psychological research. To some extent, this will contrast noticiably with the quantitative methods emphasized by our course. Even though these two approaches do differ, they are not mutually exclusive and can complement one another in many ways. Consequently, it is important to be aware of both.

The material covered in Dr. Sharp's lecture is testable and will appear on the Final Exam. Please take the time to watch the lecture carefully and engage thoughtfully with the concepts presented.

After watching the lecture and taking notes, you will need to complete a 30-minute "Knowledge Challenge" consisting of 30 multiple-choice questions based on the lecture content. Once you start, the timer will begin, and you must complete the challenge in one attempt. Multiple attempts will not be permitted. To confirm that you have read these instructions, you will need to enter the access code 55GWMn prior to beginning.

Students with approved academic accommodations (i.e., extended time) will have their adjustments applied to this assessment.
