Sample Exam

This sample exam is designed to help you become familiar with the style and format of actual exam questions. However, it is not a substitute for thorough studying. To succeed, it's important to create your own study materials and make effective use of the resources provided. Taking an active role in your preparation will not only deepen your understanding but also build the confidence you need for the exam.

  1. In this course, the exams come with time constraints. What should you do immediately if you encounter a question you don’t know or need to ‘think about’ during the exam?


    1. Panic.
    2. Sit and think about what the answer should be.
    3. Flag the question, choose an answer swiftly, and then proceed to the next one. You can revisit and spend extra time on it later if needed.
    4. Look up the answer.

  2. Madison is preparing for her upcoming exam by taking practice quizzes online. Each time she takes the quiz she is presented with 10 randomly chosen questions, and every time she receives a grade of 80%. These results suggest that the quiz is…


    1. valid
    2. reliable
    3. correlated
    4. empirical
    5. invalid

  3. The “ancient alien hypothesis” suggests that extraterrestrial beings visited Earth in ancient times and influenced human culture and technology. Why is this idea not considered a robust scientific theory?


    1. It is often discussed in popular media.
    2. It lacks testable predictions and verifiable evidence.
    3. It challenges conventional archaeology.
    4. The scientific community is corrupt and suppresses the truth.
    5. It suggests ancient civilizations used advanced technology.

  4. In a study examining the effects of stress (stress group vs. no stress group) on memory performance, _____ is the independent variable and _____ is the dependent variable.


    1. stress group 2; stress group 1
    2. stress vs. no stress; memory performance
    3. stress group 1; stress group 2
    4. number of groups; items correctly recalled
    5. memory performance; stress vs. no stress

  5. René argues with her friend Kevin that what goes on in the mind is fundamentally different and removed from what happens in the body. René’s views are in line with which philosophical approach?


    1. mentalism
    2. idealism
    3. realism
    4. materialism
    5. dualism

  6. Dr. Nelson is a psychologist who interested in studying aggression in sports. For her research, she attends high school basketball games and records the number of aggressive acts she observes. Dr. Nelson’s research is best viewed as meeting psychology’s basic goal of ________.


    1. Control
    2. Understanding
    3. Prediction
    4. Description

  7. When interpreting a correlation coefficient …


    1. A negative value suggests a weak or no relationship between variables.
    2. The closer the coefficient is to +1 or -1, the stronger the relationship between variables.
    3. A correlation of 0 implies no relationship between variables.
    4. B and C
    5. All of the above are correct.

  8. Victoria thinks she knows what Professor Harsen’s study is about, so she responds in a way that she thinks will help him to support his hypothesis. It is likely that there are some _____ that are influencing Victoria’s behaviour.


    1. manipulations
    2. samples
    3. operational definitions
    4. theories
    5. demand characteristics

  9. A psychoanalytic psychologist assumes that people with unresolved childhood issues are more susceptible to stress and anxiety. The psychologist’s assumption is best viewed as an example of:


    1. a hypothesis
    2. a theory
    3. correlational research
    4. clinical research
    5. a scientific fact

  10. According to Structuralists, Psychology would likely be defined as the:


    1. study of consciousness
    2. study of behaviour and the mind
    3. examination of unconscious factors
    4. study of personality

  11. If we want to know how a group of 100 three-year-olds will perform on a computerized memory task and select 20 of the three-year-olds to actually do the task, then the group of 100 refers to our _____ and the group of 20 refers to our _____.


    1. population; sample
    2. experimental group; sample
    3. sample; population
    4. control group; experimental group
    5. experimental group; control group

  12. Nathan is angry and frustrated at work. He consults with a therapist who asks Nathan many detailed questions about his early childhood and interprets Nathan’s problems as being due to conflicts between unconscious aggressive urges and his defense mechanisms. Nathan’s therapist would most likely identify with which psychological perspective?
